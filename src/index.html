Astronomical Scintillation

Back at earth let's look into the night sky again. Ah yes, that looks better. So something between here and the star is causing the twinkle effect. It's not the star itself.
It's more or less the very same effect as you can see here on the ground of the pool. There a brighter and fainter areas of light.
To have such an effect you need to look at a far distant objects so they act like point sources of light. Stars are matching this definition. Our moon and our solar system planets are not far away enough so you can't see the effect.
