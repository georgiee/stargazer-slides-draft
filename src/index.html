Astronomical Scintillation

Back at earth let's look into the night sky again. Ah yes, that looks better. So something between here and the star is causing the twinkle effect. It's not the star itself.
It's more or less the very same effect as you can see here on the ground of the pool. There a brighter and fainter areas of light.
To have such an effect you need to look at a far distant objects so they act like point sources of light. Stars are matching this definition. Our moon and our solar system planets are not far away enough so you can't see the effect.

Bonus Chapter

Parallax Sky

Parallax is a displacement or difference in the apparent position of an object viewed along two different lines of sight.
👉 Look at a near object, alternately close your eyes and you see it in different positions
Thanks to this effect our brain can perceive depth with the help of our two eyes each with a different line of sight. The parallax effect comes from the fact that distance objects appear smaller and slower.
Everybody knows this effect from a car or train ride or maybe you created a parallax effect on your website.

Stellar parallax

  • calculate distance to far stars
  • very small numbers
